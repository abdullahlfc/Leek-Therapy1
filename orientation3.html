Who We Are

Leek Therapy is a clinician-owned mental health therapy group practice, offering individual therapy to our clients, in-person and via telehealth. Through collaborative, personalized care, we help individuals navigate life’s challenges, build resilience, and make meaningful change.

Who We Support

We proudly offer welcoming, tailored therapeutic spaces for the following demographics:

Children (5+) Teens Adults LGBTQ+ Individuals College Students First Responders Caregivers

What We Treat

Understanding the clinical terminology our clients and clinicians use. We treat clients with a variety of presentations. Below are some common examples of terms you will hear when assisting clients. You will be provided with a glossary of terms, so it is not required that you memorize these, but having a basic understanding will help. Click any box to read more.

Anxiety +

Constant worry, tension, and overthinking that disrupts daily life. Clients may feel overwhelmed or physically tense.

Depression +

A heavy mood causing a loss of motivation, profound sadness, and a feeling of disconnection from parts of life that used to feel meaningful.

PTSD & Trauma +

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder. Lingering distress from painful experiences. Therapy helps process these memories at a manageable pace to reduce their grip on daily life.

Neurodivergent Support +

ADHD & ASD. Affirming care for Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder and Autism Spectrum Disorder. Focuses on understanding brain patterns to create plans for regulation and daily functioning.

LGBTQIA2S+ Support +

Affirming, identity-aware support that deeply respects a client's lived experience, gender identity, and sexual orientation.

BPD +

Borderline Personality Disorder. A condition impacting how a person thinks and feels about themselves and others, causing intense emotional swings and relationship instability.

Therapeutic Modalities

Similarly to the conditions we treat, you will be provided with a glossary of terms, so it is not required that you memorize these terms, but having a basic understanding of the approaches our clinicians use will help. Click any box to read more.

EMDR +

Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing. A highly structured, trauma-informed method that uses bilateral stimulation (like eye movements) to reprocess distressing memories.

CBT +

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. A practical approach focused on identifying and changing negative thought patterns to improve emotions and behaviors.

DBT +

Dialectical Behavior Therapy. Focuses on teaching skills for emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and mindfulness. Often used for extreme emotional swings.

ACT +

Acceptance & Commitment Therapy. Teaches clients to stop fighting their negative emotions and instead commit to behaviors that align with their personal values.

IFS +

Internal Family Systems. Views the mind as made up of different "parts" (e.g., an angry part, a wounded part) and helps heal them to restore mental balance.

SFBT +

Solution Focused Brief Therapy. A short-term, goal-directed therapy focused on building solutions rather than solving past problems.

Important Billing Terms & Insurance

Understanding how Leek Therapy handles billing ensures administrative clarity. Click the major billing pillars below to read their definitions.

Deductible +

The amount a client must pay entirely out-of-pocket for medical services before their insurance begins to pay anything.

Copay +

A fixed flat fee (e.g., $25) the client pays for each therapy session after their deductible is met.

In-Network +

When a therapist has a signed contract with an insurance company to provide services at a negotiated, discounted rate.

Eligibility of Benefits (VOB) +

The process our billing team goes through to check exactly what a new client's insurance will and will not cover.

Insurances We Accept:

BCBS (Blue Cross Blue Shield) Optum / UnitedHealthcare Aetna Tufts (Commercial plans) Harvard Pilgrim Medicare
