Who We Are

Leek Therapy is a clinician-owned mental health therapy group practice, offering individual therapy to our clients, in-person and via telehealth. Through collaborative, personalized care, we help individuals navigate life’s challenges, build resilience, and make meaningful change.

Who We Support Children (5+), Teens, Adults, LGBTQ+ Individuals, College Students, First Responders, Caregivers.

What We Treat

Click to expand definitions:

Anxiety ▼
Constant worry, tension, and overthinking that disrupts daily life. Clients may feel overwhelmed or physically tense.
Depression ▼
A heavy mood causing a loss of motivation, profound sadness, and a feeling of disconnection from parts of life that used to feel meaningful.
PTSD & Trauma (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder) ▼
Lingering distress from painful experiences. Therapy helps process these memories at a manageable pace to reduce their grip on daily life.
Neurodivergent Support (ADHD & ASD) ▼
Affirming care for Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder and Autism Spectrum Disorder. Focuses on understanding brain patterns to create plans for regulation and daily functioning.
LGBTQIA2S+ Support & BPD ▼
LGBTQIA2S+ Support: Affirming, identity-aware support that deeply respects a client's lived experience, gender identity, and sexual orientation.

BPD (Borderline Personality Disorder): A condition impacting how a person thinks and feels about themselves and others, causing intense emotional swings and relationship instability.
Other Conditions We Treat: Life Transitions, Bipolar Disorder, Family Conflict, Postpartum Mental Health, Grief & Loss, Substance Use Disorders, Eating Disorders, Parenting, Stress, Relationship Issues.
Slide 3

Therapeutic Modalities

Click to expand approaches:

EMDR ▼
(Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing): A highly structured, trauma-informed method that uses bilateral stimulation (like eye movements) to reprocess distressing memories.
CBT ▼
(Cognitive Behavioral Therapy): A practical approach focused on identifying and changing negative thought patterns to improve emotions and behaviors.
DBT ▼
(Dialectical Behavior Therapy): Focuses on teaching skills for emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and mindfulness. Often used for extreme emotional swings.
ACT / IFS / SFBT ▼
ACT: Teaches clients to stop fighting their negative emotions and instead commit to behaviors that align with values.

IFS: Views the mind as made up of different "parts" and helps heal them to restore mental balance.

SFBT: A short-term, goal-directed therapy focused on building solutions rather than solving past problems.
Other Modalities Offered: Somatic Therapy, MBCT- Mindfulness Based, Art Therapy, Play Therapy, Strength Based, Compassion Focused, Person-Centered.

Important Billing & Insurance Terms

Click to expand definitions:

Deductible & Copay ▼
Deductible: The amount a client must pay entirely out-of-pocket for medical services before their insurance begins to pay anything.

Copay: A fixed flat fee (e.g., $25) the client pays for each therapy session (after their deductible is met, if the client has a deductible).
In-Network & Eligibility (VOB) ▼
In-Network: When a therapist has a signed contract with an insurance company to provide services at a negotiated, discounted rate. (Note: Fallon and MBHP are not accepted).

Eligibility of Benefits (VOB): The process our billing team goes through to check if we are in-network and exactly what a new client's insurance will and will not cover.
