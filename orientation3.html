Slide 1: Introduction

Who We Are

Leek Therapy is a clinician-owned mental health therapy group practice, offering individual therapy to our clients, in-person and via telehealth. Through collaborative, personalized care, we help individuals navigate life’s challenges, build resilience, and make meaningful change.

Who We Support

Demographics: Children (5+), Teens, Adults, LGBTQ+ Individuals, College Students, First Responders, Caregivers.
Slide 2: Clinical Knowledge

What We Treat

Understanding the clinical terminology our clients and clinicians use. Below are common examples of terms you will hear when assisting clients. Having a basic understanding will help.

Anxiety Constant worry, tension, and overthinking that disrupts daily life. Clients may feel overwhelmed or physically tense. Depression A heavy mood causing a loss of motivation, profound sadness, and a feeling of disconnection from parts of life that used to feel meaningful. PTSD & Trauma (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder) Lingering distress from painful experiences. Therapy helps process these memories at a manageable pace to reduce their grip on daily life. Neurodivergent Support (ADHD & ASD) Affirming care for Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder and Autism Spectrum Disorder. Focuses on understanding brain patterns to create plans for regulation and daily functioning. LGBTQIA2S+ Support Affirming, identity-aware support that deeply respects a client's lived experience, gender identity, and sexual orientation. BPD (Borderline Personality Disorder) & OCD BPD: A condition impacting how a person thinks and feels about themselves and others, causing intense emotional swings and relationship instability.
OCD: Obsessive Compulsive Disorder.
Other Conditions We Treat:

Life Transitions, Bipolar Disorder, Family Conflict, Postpartum Mental Health, Grief & Loss, Substance Use Disorders, Eating Disorders, Parenting, Stress, Relationship Issues.
Slide 3: Approaches

Therapeutic Modalities

Similarly to the conditions we treat, you will be provided with a glossary of terms, so it is not required that you memorize these terms, but having a basic understanding of the approaches our clinicians use will help.

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing): A highly structured, trauma-informed method that uses bilateral stimulation (like eye movements) to reprocess distressing memories. CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy): A practical approach focused on identifying and changing negative thought patterns to improve emotions and behaviors. DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy): Focuses on teaching skills for emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and mindfulness. Often used for extreme emotional swings. ACT (Acceptance & Commitment Therapy): Teaches clients to stop fighting their negative emotions and instead commit to behaviors that align with their personal values. IFS (Internal Family Systems): Views the mind as made up of different "parts" (e.g., an angry part, a wounded part) and helps heal them to restore mental balance. SFBT (Solution Focused Brief Therapy): A short-term, goal-directed therapy focused on building solutions rather than solving past problems.
Other Modalities Offered:

Somatic Therapy, MBCT- Mindfulness Based, Art Therapy, Play Therapy, Strength Based, Compassion Focused, Person-Centered.
